Chronicle of Space Travel

Humanity's Journey Beyond Earth

1957
Sputnik 1 Launched
The Soviet Union launched the first artificial satellite into orbit, marking the beginning of the Space Age.
1961
First Human in Space
Yuri Gagarin became the first human to orbit Earth aboard Vostok 1, completing one orbit in 108 minutes.
1969
Moon Landing
Apollo 11 astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to walk on the Moon.
1971
First Space Station
The Soviet Union launched Salyut 1, the world's first space station, paving the way for long-duration spaceflight.
1981
Space Shuttle Era Begins
NASA's Space Shuttle Columbia completed its first mission, inaugurating the era of reusable spacecraft.
1998
International Space Station
Construction of the ISS began with the launch of the Zarya module, representing unprecedented international cooperation.
2012
Commercial Spaceflight
SpaceX's Dragon became the first commercial spacecraft to dock with the ISS, marking a new era of private space exploration.
2020
Crew Dragon Success
SpaceX successfully transported NASA astronauts to the ISS, returning crewed launches to American soil.

Exploring the cosmos, one milestone at a time.
